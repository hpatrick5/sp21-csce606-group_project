District School Performance Predictor

In the state of Texas, testing is part of the accountability system to grade schools from A to F. As part of the State of Texas Assessments of Academic Readiness (STAAR) program, students in grades three to eight take assessments in math and reading each year. School principals and leadership teams have yearned for some sort of predictability program that can help to accurately predict a student's score on the state-wide assessment.

Educational Leadership Research Center (ELRC) at Texas A&M University has worked on a machine learning model which predicts students' performance based on their percent score in the previous year STAAR tests (if available) and in practice STAAR released assessments that were taken throughout the year. Using a CSV that you can upload to our site, our machine learning predictions will help you to predict what students will score without any additional interventions.

By using this model, school leaders will save countless hours in data research and can instead focus on the needed interventions. We suggest that you use this model approximately seven to eight weeks prior to the state-wide assessment. By doing this, you will have ample time to work with all students (those close to masters, meets grade-level, approaches grade-level, or did not meet grade-level). You will also be able to look at students in fourth to eighth grades to see if the students have met their growth that is needed for the student progress domain.

After you upload your student information, you will receive a CSV file with a predicted score in the subjects you uploaded.
