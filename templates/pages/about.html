About the District School Performance Predictor

In the state of Texas, student testing is part of the accountability system used to grade schools from A to F. The State of Texas Assessments of Academic Readiness (STAAR) program is one such suite of testing in which students from grades three to eight take assessments in math and reading each year. These assessments are based on the Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills, state curriculum standards.

Therefore, school principals and leadership teams have yearned for a software that can predict student STAAR assessment scores in order to help determine where to best focus school resources. Predicting future student STAAR assessment scores in order to aid school principals’ and leadership teams’ decisions is the purpose of this website.

The Educational Leadership Research Center (ELRC) at Texas A&M University has worked on a machine learning model to predict student STAAR performances using students’ prior percent scores on STAAR tests (if available), and practice STAAR released assessments taken throughout the year. By simply uploading a CSV file with the appropriate data to our site, our returned machine learning model STAAR assessment score predictions will save you countless hours in data research, analysis, and processing. We suggest that you use this model approximately seven to eight weeks prior to the state-wide assessment. By doing this, you will have ample time to work with all students including those close to masters, meets grade-level, approaches grade-level, or did not meet grade-level. You will also be able to look at students in third to eighth grades to see if the students have met their growth that is needed for the student progress domain. After you upload your student information, you will receive a CSV file with a predicted STAAR assessment score in the subjects you uploaded.

About the Education Leadership Research Center

The Education Leadership Research Center (ELRC) was established by the Texas A&M University College of Education and Human Development. The ELRC's mission is to inform and enrich the leadership practices of individuals and school organizations at the local, state, national and international levels., and to provide cutting edge professional learning and services to teacher leaders, school administrators, and policy makers. Visit theELRC's main website for more information on their objectives, affiliations, and additional invaluable services.
